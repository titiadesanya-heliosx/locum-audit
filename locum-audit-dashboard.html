⏱ Hours discrepancy
Compares each locum's Hubstaff logged hours (what they claim to have worked) against their active hours — the number of distinct hours in which they made at least one clinical decision. Any hour they were clocked in but made no decisions counts as inactive paid time. Only locums where inactive hours exceed 20% of their logged hours are shown. The hourly grid for each day shows green (decisions made), red (clocked in, no decisions). Note: activity timestamps are hourly so the active count may underestimate by up to 1 hour per day.

🚨 Off-rota order types
These locums made decisions on order types outside their rota assignment. They are contracted to work on Complex Repeats, Simple Repeats, and Periodic Reviews only. Working on Starter Doses or Transfer Patient orders is a compliance issue — on the first offence a formal warning is issued; on the second offence the locum is immediately offboarded.

💊 Prescribe rate below benchmark
The prescribe rate is the proportion of orders a locum approves (prescribes) out of all orders they reviewed. Internal benchmarks are ≥60% for Complex Repeats (including 6-Month Reviews) and ≥98% for Simple Repeats. A low prescribe rate — particularly on Simple Repeats where the vast majority should be straightforward — may indicate excessive caution, inconsistent clinical judgement, or deliberate deflection of decisions via holds. Only locums below benchmark on at least one order type are shown.
Name Complex rate Simple rate Total decisions Flag Action
⏸ High hold rate
A "hold" means the locum did not make a final prescribing decision on an order — they flagged it for further review instead. While some holds are clinically appropriate, a consistently high hold rate relative to peers can indicate a locum is avoiding decisions rather than completing their work. Flagged where hold rate exceeds the 75th percentile of the group this week, with a minimum of 5 decisions reviewed.
